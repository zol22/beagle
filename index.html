Beagle Overview

Beagles are among America's favorite dogs. They're very vocal. In fact, the name “beagle” probably comes from an old French word for “gaped throat. Once used as a hunting companion , the Beagle is a friendly and cheerful family companion

Questions

What kind of temperament and personality does the Beagle have?

The temperament of a Beagle is notoriously friendly and gentle. They are even-tempered dogs, neither too aggressive nor too timid. They have medium energy and are of a smaller build. Other Beagles traits are “happy-go-lucky,” and loyal. They also are known for their soulful expressions.

What colors do Beagles come in?

The vast majority of Beagles are some combination of white, brown, and black, either in a tricolor (most common) or bicolor pattern.

How much grooming do Beagles need?

Just occasional brushing to keep dirt and dust out of their dense coat. During shedding season , you should brush your Beagle every day to pull out the worst of the dead hair so less of it ends up on your floor and furniture.

Do Beagles have a doggy odor?

Yes, all hounds have a doggy odor and it can be quite strong.

Find a Beagle

Beagles are popular and well-loved as family dogs all over the country. Initially bred to be a hunting and tracking dog, Beagles have incredibly powerful noses, short legs that keep them near their scent, and an impressive intellect to help them track. Known for their loud “voices” and curious, happy personalities, Beagles tend to be very healthy dogs. They are eager to please and extremely curious, especially when they catch an interesting scent! They make wonderful, amusing companions
